This Easter break I've been visiting Budapest together with my in-laws. 
The main goal of the trip was to celebrate my father-in-law's retirement. 
As expected, the city was everything that someone can image of a centre-European-well-history-nourished city: breath-taking views from and of the river, typical food rich in calories, large glasses of beer, etc. 
However, there was something that indeed pissed me off. A part from a small inscription in his birthplace I did`t find any reference to the life and work of John Von Neuman. 
Considering that among his achievement one can found solid and fundamental contributions not only to mathematics but to other cientific field such as computer science or economics, I coudn't find no more that a brief description of his life not even wrotte by the townhall but by and association of mathematics.
